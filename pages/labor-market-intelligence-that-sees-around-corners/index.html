Why Traditional Labour Market Intelligence Is Misleading

Industry experts agree: most labour market tools rely on outdated, lagging indicators that mislead more than they inform.

Most workforce analytics tools are built to look backward. They scrape job boards, analyse outdated salary data, and count how often a skill appears in resumes. But these signals are slow, noisy, and often wrong.

Job postings reflect old needs—not future requirements.

Salary ranges are distorted—by geography, tenure, and company size.

Resumes show up late—a skill may not appear until years after someone begins using it.
